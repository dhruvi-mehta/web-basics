BOMBAY (MUMBAI)

Bombay, now known as Mumbai, will always be Bombay to me. There's a sense of comfort in using the name that my parents have always called it, a name tied to their stories, memories, and the home they knew. While the city has evolved and embraced its modern identity as Mumbai, calling it Bombay feels like holding onto a piece of my family's history, a connection to the past that grounds me every time I return.I moved away from Bombay when I was just five years old, but I’ve often visited over the years. Although I don't have clear memories of living there, each visit feels like an opportunity to rediscover the city and make new memories. There's a comfort in returning to the place where my roots lie, surrounded by family and familiar landmarks. Yet, at the same time, I feel a certain disconnect—the city changes with every visit, evolving in ways I don't always recognize or relate to. It’s a bittersweet feeling, as Bombay will always be home in my heart, yet it grows distant with time.

Things I do everytime I visit Bombay

  1. Visit my paternal grandmotherdadi : As soon as my family lands in Bombay, we head straight to my dadi's house (paternal grandmother in many Indian languages), where she always prepares the most delicious meals.
  2. Food Staples: My family and I always crave certain staples that we can’t easily find in Singapore or, for me, here in New York. So, we always have a long list of foods we want to eat, like Vada Pav,Bombay grill sandwich, and cold coffee made with blended vanilla ice cream, coffee, and a hint of cardamom.
  3. Enjoy fresh coconut water straight from the tree at my grandparents' house: I love coconuts! Every time I visit Bombay, my grandmother makes sure someone gets fresh coconuts from the tree and cuts them up for us to enjoy. It's so refreshing, especially on those warmer days!
  4. Sitting in a rickshaw: One of my favorite ways to experience the city, with the wind in my hair, the hustle of the streets around me, and the sights of Bombay passing by.
  5. Coffee date with one of my favorite cousins: One of my cousins and I are really close, and over the years we’ve started this tradition of meeting super early in the morning to walk by the water at Bandstand and grab coffee afterward. I love our morning hangouts because it’s so calm and quiet, and it’s the best time to catch up and just be together.

Food I Want to Eat everytime I visit Bombay

  • Vada Pav - A classic Mumbai street food, crispy potato fritter sandwiched between soft buns, served with chutneys.
  • Pani Puri - A burst of flavors with crispy puris filled with tangy and spicy water, tamarind chutney, and spiced potatoes.
  • Pav Bhaji - A delicious blend of mashed vegetables cooked in butter and spices, served with buttered buns.
  • Dosa A crispy, thin South Indian pancake served with sambar and coconut chutney.
  • Misal Pav Misal Pav - A spicy curry made with moth beans, topped with crunchy farsan (fried snacks) and served with bread rolls (pav).
